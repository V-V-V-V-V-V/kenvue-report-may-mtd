01 · The month in brief

April delivered exactly the kind of launch month the always-on model is built for.

Month 1 of the 3-month campaign closed at $87,105 invested — within the planned $92K Month 1 envelope (20% of the $479K total) — across five channels and seven priority markets. The campaign generated 62.5M impressions, 1.83M clicks, 25.7M unique people reached on Meta, and 1,078 tracked apply starts. Click-through rate (2.93%) and cost-per-click ($0.048) both substantially outperformed employer-brand category benchmarks.

"$87,105 invested. 1.83 million career-content visits. 25.7 million unique people reached. The Kenvue talent message is at full operating scale across every priority market."

What this means

The campaign is in market, at scale, and engaging audiences across every region. The Kenvue talent narrative is reaching its intended communities — and the data shows real signal in how it's landing. Against typical employer-brand benchmarks (CTR 0.6–1.2%, CPC $0.30–$0.80), the campaign is operating in the top decile of efficiency.

Google Display alone delivered 1.30M clicks at $0.018 CPC — exceptional by any standard. Meta reached 25.7 million unique people across the seven markets at sub-$1 CPM. Month 2 is positioned to compound on this strong foundation with sharper creative, deeper placement optimisation, and refined function-level investment.

Bottom line: Month 1 has validated the channel mix, market split, and creative approach. The campaign exits April with a working playbook, healthy efficiency margins, and clear levers to compound performance heading into Month 2.
On-budget delivery: Month 1 closed at $87,105 against the $92K planned envelope (20% of total $479K, per the 20/35/45 monthly cadence). The remaining ~$5K of headroom carries into Month 2, where the planned spend rises to ~$167K (35% of total) before Month 3 closes the campaign with the largest spend month (~$216K · 45% of total).

03 · How the month evolved

The campaign built into peak delivery in Weeks 2–3, then deliberately scaled back.

Week 1 focused on launch and audience signal establishment. By Weeks 2–3 all platforms and campaigns were fully active and spending, reaching peak delivery. From Week 3 (post 20 April) we began a deliberate scale-down of Google Display and Meta specifically, to preserve budget headroom for Month 2's larger allocation.

10 · Optimisation opportunities

Four opportunities to sharpen Month 2.

Each opportunity below builds on Month 1's strong foundation. The geographic spend allocation continues as planned — these recommendations focus on creative, channel, function, and operational optimisations that compound the existing performance.

Opportunity 1 · Channel mix

Lean further into Google Display.

Display is the most efficient click engine in the mix at $0.018 CPC and 6.74% CTR. There's clear runway to grow Display's share of the channel mix in Month 2 without breaking efficiency. Recommended: increase Display allocation by ~15% within the existing market-level budgets.

Scale what's working
Opportunity 2 · Function mix

Increase weight on Manufacturing, Operations, and Marketing function pools.

These three functions delivered 5–7% CTR — well above the campaign average — on small budgets. Modest budget increases here would compound the high engagement signal without disturbing the larger Marketing & Sales pool.

Function-level optimisation
Opportunity 3 · Creative refresh

Refresh top-performing Meta creative units and add seniority-targeted LinkedIn variants.

Meta creative has been live for the full month — refreshing the top 2 units in Month 2 will protect against fatigue as spend scales. On LinkedIn, CXO/VP audiences engaged at 1.4% CTR (highest of all seniority bands) — a senior-targeted creative variant could compound that signal.

Creative direction
Opportunity 4 · Placement weighting

Weight more deliberately into Stories and Native placements on Meta.

Facebook Stories (4.48% CTR) and Native/banner (3.84% CTR) are the highest-engaging Meta placements but received modest spend share. A deliberate weighting toward these formats in Month 2 could lift overall Meta CTR by ~30bps.

Placement optimisation
Geographic spend allocation continues as planned: NA at 34% (USA), EMEA at 35% (FR/DE/UK), APAC at 18% (India), and LATAM at 12% (BR/CO). The optimisations above operate within those market-level budgets to compound performance — they do not shift budget across markets.
